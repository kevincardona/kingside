# App name options

The app can't ship as just "Chess" (generic, unsearchable, almost certainly
taken). Below are candidates with a clear recommendation. App Store listings
are **Name + Subtitle** — the name is the brand, the subtitle carries keywords.

## ✅ FINAL CHOICE — Kingside (locked)

**Kingside** — a real chess term (kingside castling): recognisable, ≤12 chars,
and (confirmed by an App Store search) with **no competing "Kingside" chess app**.
- Icon label / display name: **Kingside** (set via `config/name` in `project.godot`)
- App Store name: `Kingside – Offline Chess`
- Subtitle: `Bots, puzzles & game review`
- Why not the others: *Gambit* ("Gambit Chess" + a "Gambit" game are both live),
  *Caïssa*, *Knightly*, *Tempo* ("Chess Tempo"), *Caelum* ("Caelum Inc" makes a
  chess app) are all taken/crowded. Reserve the exact string in App Store Connect
  to confirm uniqueness before submitting.

## Naming guidance
- Keep the **name ≤ 12 chars** so it isn't truncated under the icon.
- Put real keywords ("chess", "puzzles", "offline") in the **subtitle**, not the name.
- Check availability: App Store Connect (name uniqueness) + a quick trademark
  search before committing.

**Updated pick (after the availability check): Kingside.** It's clean, on-theme,
≤12 chars, and the only researched candidate without a direct App Store
competitor — *Gambit*, *Caïssa* and *Knightly* are all already taken by chess
apps. Suggested listing: name `Kingside – Offline Chess`, subtitle
`Bots, puzzles & game review`. The marketing files use *Gambit* as a placeholder,
so swap that one string to change it everywhere. **Confirm the exact name is free
in App Store Connect before committing.**
